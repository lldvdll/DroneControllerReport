\section{Results}
We evaluate controllers over 100 episodes with 5 random targets (Table \ref{tab:results_bounded}). Performance is assessed by \textit{Target Hits} (average acquisitions), \textit{Crash Rate} (boundary collision\%), \textit{Travel Time} (median steps per target), and \textit{Path Efficiency} (ratio of path length to Euclidean distance). In the bounded test, the physics-based heuristic controller achieves the fastest travel time (423 steps) but exhibits a high crash rate (46\%). \textbf{SARSA Slow} prioritises safety with the lowest crash rate (22\%) through a cautious approach at the cost of speed. \textbf{SARSA Fast} balances speed and safety, achieving the best path efficiency (1.24) and a 34.00\% crash rate. It also demonstrates excellent generalisation to the out-of-sample unconstrained scenario, achieving 7.8 out of 10 target acquisition, 28\% more than heuristic (not shown). As expected, the REINFORCE controller achieved only 0.36 target hits, though (not shown) invariably scores highest on stability measures. This suggests that for bounded, unstable dynamics, tabular SARSA can provide robust policy learning despite its discrete nature.
